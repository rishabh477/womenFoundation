Impact

We advance women’s prosperity with our community, philanthropy, and our statewide WAGES program.

  • Research
  • Public Policy Advocacy
  • Grantee Cohort
  • Collaborations

Research

High-quality research shines the way forward for WFCO, highlighting underlying causes of gender inequity and informing our grantmaking and advocacy work. Together with our partners, WFCO has released more than 30 research reports since our inception, including four reports since 2017 that have become invaluable resources for the community in creating change for women in Colorado.

Public Policy Advocacy

Public policy is one of the most powerful tools for creating systemic change. One bill can transform the lives of hundreds of thousands of women and their families. Since 1987, WFCO and our partners have advanced nearly 40 measures that address root causes of poverty and promote economic security.

Civic engagement is critical to systemic change, you can use your voice to transform your community! Read The Womanifesto, our nonpartisan voter handbook for everyone who cares about strengthening Colorado, and use our civic engagement guide to make a difference for women, families, communities, and the economy.

Grantee Cohort

Our WAGES grantee cohort propels more women into careers that enable them to meet the current and future needs of their families. From Denver to Durango and Fort Collins to Fort Morgan, our 23 grantee partners address the needs of communities statewide by working with women and their children at the same time to build a foundation for family security for generations to come.

Collaborations

When it comes to breaking through barriers, paving roads to opportunities, and helping every woman thrive, we know we can’t do it alone. WFCO is proud to be part of a rising movement of diverse partners who share this goal and know that we can do more together by collaborating.

DID YOU KNOW?

WAGES (Women Achieving Greater Economic Security) is our programmatic body of work that employs research, public policy advocacy, and strategic grantmaking to help Colorado women reach economic security.

The aim of WAGES is to impact Colorado’s prosperity by propelling more women into careers that enable them to meet the current and future needs of their families. We do this by learning and working with our partners to elevate practices that work and dismantle systems that continue to hold women back.
